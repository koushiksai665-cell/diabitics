The Diabetes Pedigree Function (DPF) measures genetic diabetes risk based on your relatives' history. Tick each blood relative who has or had Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes, and enter their age at diagnosis. The DPF will be automatically calculated using the Smith et al. pedigree scoring formula.

How DPF is calculated: Each relative contributes a genetic weight based on relatedness (parent = 0.5, sibling = 0.5, grandparent = 0.25, uncle/aunt = 0.25, half-sibling = 0.25, cousin = 0.125). Weights are adjusted by the relative's age at diagnosis — earlier onset increases the score. The final DPF = Σ(weight × age_factor) / patient_age_factor, clamped to [0.08, 2.42] per the PIMA dataset range.

If no family history: DPF defaults to 0.078 (minimum, lowest genetic risk).
